Room 1 of 6

The Lab

📋 YOUR CLUE

On the wall, a poster reads: "I am a disturbance made of two fields at right angles to each other. I need no air, no water, no material to travel. I move at 300,000 km per second. I am everywhere — in your phone, in your microwave, in sunlight. What am I?"

What is the poster describing?

✅ EM waves are oscillating electric and magnetic fields that travel through a vacuum at c = 3×10⁸ m/s. Unlike sound, they need no medium — that's why light from the Sun can cross empty space to reach us.

Room 3 of 6

The Energy Chamber

📋 YOUR CLUE

The door has a 4-digit keypad. A whiteboard reads: "A wave has a frequency of 5×10¹⁴ Hz. The speed of light is 3×10⁸ m/s. Find the wavelength in nanometres and enter it as a whole number. (1 m = 10⁹ nm)"

💡 Hint: Use c = f × λ, so λ = c ÷ f. Then convert metres to nanometres by multiplying by 10⁹.

Enter the wavelength (in nm):

✅ λ = c ÷ f = (3×10⁸) ÷ (5×10¹⁴) = 6×10⁻⁷ m = 600 nm. This is in the visible light range (orange-red light). The relationship c = fλ shows that higher frequency means shorter wavelength.

The Spectra Vault

📋 YOUR CLUE

Three locked containers each hold part of the vault key. Each container is labelled with a spectra clue. Answer all three to collect all key parts.

KEY PART 1 OF 3

✅ Emission spectra show bright coloured lines on a black background — electrons releasing energy as photons. Absorption spectra show dark lines on a rainbow — electrons absorbing specific photons to jump to higher energy levels. Every element has a unique spectral fingerprint.
